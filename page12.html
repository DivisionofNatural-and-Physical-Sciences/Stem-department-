Lane College Pharmacy Technician - Career Pathways Certificate

This program is part of the Lane College–CVS Health partnership to ensure occupational training and employment opportunities are accessible for Lane College students and the surrounding community. The mission is to provide real-life work experience and enhance employability for careers in retail and wholesale pharmacies, hospitals, nursing homes, and assisted-living facilities. Students learn pharmacy assisting basics including pharmacy technician duties, merchandising, customer service, prescription preparation, pharmacy law, mathematics, and pharmacology.

The program utilizes a CVS mock pharmacy facility (CVS Workforce Innovation Talent Center, WITC) to provide simulated pharmacy training. The aim is to prepare graduates to sit for the national Pharmacy Technician Certification Exam (PTCE) and to provide a foundation for further study in related fields. The course is a 25 semester-credit hour certificate program, with probable completion time over two semesters. The program is recognized by the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board (PTCB).

Program Credentials and Curriculum

The pharmacy technician training program is hybrid with dedicated faculty support. CVS enriches the curriculum with hands-on learning and requires students to shadow a pharmacist or technician in an active pharmacy environment and participate in professional mentorship.

To graduate with a Certificate of Completion, students must successfully complete the following 25 credit hours.

Program Requirements

  1. Complete the Lane College admission procedure. Admission to the college does not guarantee enrollment in the Pharmacy Technician Program.
  2. Submit a Pharmacy Technician Application form to the program. (Application forms are available from the program office.)

Upon successful completion, students receive a Certificate of Completion and are eligible to take the National Pharmacy Technician Certification Examination (PTCE) administered by the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board (PTCB). PTCB certification is not required for employment as a pharmacy technician in Tennessee. For more information, see the PTCB website at www.ptcb.org.

PTCB Certification Eligibility

  • High school diploma or equivalent (e.g., GED).
  • Full disclosure of all criminal and State Board of Pharmacy registration or licensure actions; compliance with PTCB policies.
  • Passing score on the Pharmacy Technician Certification Exam (PTCE).

Note: PTCB reserves the right to investigate criminal background and verify candidate eligibility. Students should review PTCB policies before applying.
