Chemistry

Explore the fascinating world of chemistry through our comprehensive program that combines theoretical knowledge with hands-on laboratory experience. Our Chemistry Department prepares students for successful careers in research, industry, healthcare, and education while developing critical thinking and analytical problem-solving skills.

Role

The role of the Chemistry Department is to develop the ability of students to apply the techniques of critical analysis to the solution of problems. The Department strives to prepare majors with a thorough and rigorous background in chemistry.

Objectives

At the completion of the program, graduates of Chemistry will be able to:

  • Apply principles and theories of Chemistry.
  • Develop habits of accurate notation and evaluation of laboratory data.
  • Become successful in graduate and professional schools, and careers in the industry and teaching profession.
  • Understand and develop an appreciation of the significance of chemical phenomena in our daily lives.

Requirements for the Major in Chemistry

Major Requirements: A major in Chemistry consists of 42 semester hours of Biology, Chemistry, Mathematics, and Physics courses. Ten (10) 4-credit hour courses; and a 1-credit hour seminar as indicated on the curriculum chart.

The Chemistry Department also offers a pharmacy technician track and pharmacy technician certification.

Chemistry majors may select a minor from one of the following areas: Biology, Mathematics, Physics, or Computer Science.

Requirements for the Minor in Chemistry

Minor Requirements: A minor in Chemistry shall consist of 16 hours of Chemistry with a minimum grade of "C" in each course. The required courses are: CHE 141, CHE 142, CHE 243, and CHE 244.
