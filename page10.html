Course Offerings

This page lists course offerings, schedules, and descriptions for the Division of Natural and Physical Sciences departments. Each course card contains essential information including credit hours, detailed descriptions, and offering schedules.

Biology

BIO 131: Biological Science 3 Credits

A survey course introducing principles of biology through a study of the plant and animal kingdoms. Emphasis will be placed on cellular biology, gross anatomy, reproduction, heredity, and ecology. This course is designed to meet the needs of non-science majors. Three lectures per week. This class is unsuitable for STEM majors.

Offered: F/S
BIO 141/142: General Biology I, II 4 Credits

A study of the fundamental principles of biology, followed by a survey of three domains of life. Emphasis is on the chemical basis of life, cellular structure and function, energy transformations, storage and transfer of genetic information, evolution, and biodiversity. The two semesters must be taken in sequence, with completion of BIO 141 with a C or better required for BIO 142. Three lectures and one laboratory experience per week.

Offered: F/S
BIO 143: Biological Science for Education 4 Credits

A survey course introducing principles of biology through a study of the plant and animal kingdoms. Emphasis will be placed on the scientific method, cellular biology, reproduction, heredity, and ecology. Designed for non-science majors. Three lectures per week and one laboratory experience per week. This class is unsuitable for STEM majors.

Offered: S
BIO 221: Biology Laboratory Techniques and Data Analysis 2 Credits

Lab-based course offering hands-on experiences with common techniques in the biology laboratory. Practice using equipment such as the microscope and microcentrifuge; teams plan and execute a research project using suitable wet-lab and instrumental techniques.

Offered: F/S A/E/O
BIO 225: Scientific Writing 2 Credits

Develop students' skills in scientific writing. Intensive writing on scientific topics with detailed feedback on grammar and clarity. Prerequisite: ENG132.

Offered: (A) F/S
BIO 241: Comparative Vertebrate Anatomy 4 Credits

Study of the evolutionary development of organ systems of vertebrates, including complete dissections. Three lectures and one laboratory per week. Prerequisites: BIO 141 and 142.

Offered: As needed
BIO 242: Embryology 4 Credits

Descriptive approach to germ cell formation, fertilization, and development of organ systems with reference to experimental morphogenesis. Three lectures and one laboratory per week. Prerequisites: BIO 141 and BIO 142.

Offered: S / As needed
BIO 243: Molecular Biology 4 Credits

Topics include genome organization and mechanisms controlling DNA replication and repair, transcription, and protein synthesis in prokaryotes and eukaryotes. Emphasis on experimental design and data analysis with readings from primary literature. Prerequisites: BIO 141 and BIO 142.

Offered: F/S (A)
BIO 244: Plant Biology 4 Credits

Study of plant structure, function, evolution, and cultural applications. Three hours discussion and two hours laboratory per week. Prerequisites: BIO 141 and 142.

Offered: As needed
BIO 315 / BIO 316: Biology Seminars I-II 1 Credit

Students present oral and written reports on recent advances and problems in biology. One hour per week. Recommended to take one seminar at a time. Prerequisites: BIO 142, BIO 225, junior status.

Offered: (A) F/S
BIO 341: Ecology and Environmental Biology 4 Credits

Study of organism-environment interactions from an ecological perspective. Four lectures per week. Prerequisite: BIO 131 or BIO 142.

Offered: F / As needed
BIO 343: Genetics 4 Credits

Study of principles of heredity with emphasis on Mendelian genetics and recent developments. Three lectures and one laboratory per week. Prerequisites: BIO 141 and BIO 142.

Offered: S / As needed
BIO 344 / BIO 345: Human Anatomy and Physiology I & II 4 Credits

Study of human body structure and function. Designed for Biology and Health & Physical Education majors. Three lectures and one laboratory per week. Taken in sequence; BIO 344 prerequisite for BIO 345. Prerequisites: BIO 141/142.

Offered: S/F
BIO 346: Parasitology 4 Credits

Study of morphology, life cycles, and distribution of parasitic organisms. Three lectures and one laboratory per week. Prerequisites: BIO 141/142.

Offered: As needed
BIO 347: General Microbiology 4 Credits

Study of microorganisms' morphology, classification, physiology, and applications to fermentation, food, soil fertility, disease, and industry. Three lectures and one laboratory per week. Prerequisites: CHE 141/142 and BIO 141/142.

Offered: F / As needed
BIO 348 / BIO 348L: Evolution 4 Credits

Topics include microevolution, macroevolution, speciation, and evolutionary medicine. Prerequisites: BIO 141 and BIO 142 or BIO 131 with instructor permission.

Offered: As needed
BIO 425 / BIO 426: Undergraduate Research I & II 2 Credits

Research experience with hands-on training; writing-intensive with laboratory notebooks and oral/written reports. Minimum 6 hours/week per 2 credit hours. Prerequisites: BIO 142, BIO 225, consent of instructor.

Offered: F/S / As needed
BIO 442: Cell Physiology 4 Credits

Study of physiochemical properties of protoplasm and chemical dynamics of life. Three lectures and one laboratory per week. Prerequisites: BIO 141/142 and CHE 243/244.

Offered: S / As needed
BIO 443: Biochemistry 4 Credits

Key concepts in biochemistry including energy transfer, metabolism, structure-function relationships, and laboratory emphasis on measurement and quantitative analysis. Three lectures and one laboratory per week. Prerequisites: BIO 141/142; CHE 243/244.

Offered: S / As needed
BIO 444: Contemporary Issues in Experimental Biology 4 Credits

Methods, techniques, and responsible conduct of biological research; data collection, interpretation, and evaluation; topical issues explored at instructor discretion. Prerequisites: BIO 141/142 or equivalent; senior status or consent.

Offered: F / As needed
BIO 445: Human Genetics 4 Credits

Study of human genetic inheritance, gene organization, chromosome structure, karyotyping, prenatal diagnosis, and recombinant DNA technology applications. Prerequisites: BIO 141, BIO 142, and BIO 343 or permission of instructor.

Offered: As needed

Chemistry

CHE 110: Scientific Literacy and Critical Problem Solving — 1 Credit
Introduces strategies for navigating scientific problems and encourages critical thinking and problem solving. Co-requisite CHE 141. Offered: F/S (A)
CHE 141: General Chemistry I — 4 Credits
Fundamental chemical principles with applications to atomic and molecular theories, periodic trends, and properties of matter. Three lectures and one laboratory per week. Co-requisites: MAT 125/126. Offered: Fall (A)
CHE 142: General Chemistry II — 4 Credits
Continuation of CHE 141 covering thermo-chemistry, kinetics, equilibrium, electrochemistry, and introductory organic chemistry. Three lectures and one laboratory per week. Prerequisite: CHE 141. Offered: S (A)
CHE 243 / CHE 244: Organic Chemistry I & II — 4 Credits
Systematic study of major classes of organic compounds with emphasis on structure, properties, preparation, and reaction mechanisms. Three lectures and one laboratory per week. Prerequisite: CHE 142. Offered: F/S (A)
CHE 310: Special Topics in Chemistry — 3 Credits
Variable topics such as computational chemistry, environmental chemistry, or nano-science. Prerequisite: CHE 142, MAT 136. Offered: F/S (A)
CHE 311: Chemistry Seminar I — 1 Credit
Student presentations and laboratory/industry visits. Prerequisite: Junior status and consent. Offered: F/S (A)
CHE 320: Undergraduate Research in Chemistry I — 2 Credits
Selected research projects under supervision of chemistry faculty. Prerequisite: Junior status and consent. Offered: F (A) or as needed
CHE 331 / CHE 332: Chemistry Internship / Practicum I & II — 3 Credits
Workplace training with individualized learning plans; CHE 332 includes Pharmacy Technician certification review. Prerequisite: CHE 331 for CHE 332. Offered: F / As needed
CHE 343: Pharmaceutical Chemistry — 4 Credits
Introduction to drug discovery and development with emphasis on synthesis and analysis of pharmaceutical agents. Prerequisite: CHE 244. Offered: As needed
CHE 345 / CHE 346: Quantitative Analysis I & II — 4 Credits
Theories and practices of analytical chemistry with gravimetric, volumetric, electro-analytical and optical methods. Prerequisites: CHE 142 and MAT 133 (for CHE 345); CHE 345 (for CHE 346). Offered: F / S or as needed
CHE 420: Undergraduate Research in Chemistry II — 2 Credits
Continuation of CHE 320 research projects. Prerequisite: CHE 320. Offered: As needed
CHE 440: Instrumental Methods of Analysis — 4 Credits
Principles and practices of absorption and emission spectroscopy and electro-analytical techniques. Three lectures and one laboratory per week. Prerequisite: CHE 346. Offered: F (E)
CHE 445: Advanced Organic Chemistry — 4 Credits
Electronic interpretation of organic reactions, mechanisms, organometallics, and spectrophotometric techniques. Three lectures and one laboratory per week. Prerequisites: CHE 243 and CHE 244. Offered: S (O)
CHE 446: Advanced Inorganic Chemistry — 4 Credits
Quantum theory, atomic structure, bonding, coordination compounds, and periodic properties. Three lectures and one laboratory per week. Prerequisites: CHE 345 and MAT 241. Offered: F (O)
CHE 447: Physical Chemistry I — 4 Credits
Laws and theories of gases, liquids, solids with introductions to thermodynamics and kinetics. Three lectures and one laboratory per week. Prerequisites: CHE 345, MAT 240. Offered: F (O) or as needed
CHE 448: Physical Chemistry II — 4 Credits
Introduction to quantum mechanics, spectroscopy, and computational modeling of atoms. Three lectures and one laboratory per week. Prerequisites: CHE 345, PHY 241, MAT 241. Offered: S (O) or as needed

CSC 237: Computer Systems and Assembly Language — 3 Credits

CSC 239: Visual Programming — 3 Credits

CSC 332: Data Structures and Algorithms — 3 Credits

CSC 333: Computer Graphics — 3 Credits

CSC 334: Information Systems and Design — 3 Credits

CSC 336: Software Engineering and Project Management — 3 Credits

CSC 339: Database Management Systems I — 3 Credits

CSC 433 / CSC 434: Operating Systems I & II — 3 Credits

CSC 435: Survey of Programming Languages — 3 Credits

CSC 439: Senior Project — 3 Credits

Mathematics

MAT 133: College Algebra — 3 Credits

MAT 230: Discrete Mathematics — 3 Credits

MAT 233: Probability and Statistics — 3 Credits

MAT 240 / MAT 241 / MAT 340 — Calculus I, II, III — 4 Credits each
Calculus sequence covering limits, differentiation, integration, series, multivariable calculus, and applications. Prerequisite progression applies. Offered: F/S
MAT 242: Linear Algebra — 3 Credits

Physics

PHY 131: Physical Science — 3 Credits
Survey of physical science topics including principles of physics, chemistry, meteorology, astronomy, and geology. Designed for non-science majors. Three lectures per week. Offered: F/S (A)
PHY 132: Introduction to Astronomy — 3 Credits
Survey concepts in astronomy including solar system, stars, galaxies, and cosmology. Three lectures per week. Offered: F (O)
PHY 133: Galileo and the Church — 3 Credits
Exploration of historical and scientific issues around Galileo's trials, including lab work on telescopes and parallax. Cross-listed as REL 133. Offered: S (E)
PHY 134: Light, Color, and Vision — 3 Credits
Introduction to optics and vision with laboratory demonstrations. Offered: F (E)
PHY 135: Physics of Music — 3 Credits
Study of sound generation, perception, and recording with laboratory demonstrations. Offered: S (O)
PHY 241 / PHY 242: Physics I & II — 4 Credits each
Classical mechanics, waves, thermodynamics (PHY 241) and electromagnetism, circuits, optics, and modern physics introductions (PHY 242). Three lectures and one laboratory per week. Prerequisites apply. Offered: F/S
PHY 251: Modern Physics — 4 Credits
Relativity, quantum theory of light, wave nature of matter, and introduction to quantum mechanics. Prerequisite: PHY 242. Offered: F / As needed
PHY 301: Math Methods of Physics — 3 Credits
Applied mathematics techniques for physics and chemistry including linear algebra and ODEs. Prerequisite: MAT 240. Offered: S / As needed
PHY 331: Classical Mechanics — 3 Credits
Statics and dynamics, Lagrange/Hamiltonian formalisms, fluids, and vibrations. Prerequisites: PHY 242 and PHY 301. Offered: S / As needed
PHY 333: Electronics — 4 Credits
Introduction to electronic devices and circuits with laboratories. Prerequisite: PHY 242. Offered: S / As needed
PHY 335: Thermal Physics — 3 Credits
Equation of state, laws of thermodynamics, kinetic theory, and statistical mechanics. Prerequisite: PHY 242. Offered: F / As needed
PHY 345: Electromagnetism — 3 Credits
Vector analysis, Maxwell's equations, and wave propagation. Prerequisites: PHY 242 and PHY 301. Offered: F / As needed
PHY 411: Physics Seminar — 1 Credit
Research presentations on current topics in physics for juniors and seniors. Prerequisite: PHY 242. Offered: S / As needed
PHY 435: Optics — 3 Credits
Modern optics including geometrical and wave optics with applications to spectroscopy and lasers. Prerequisite: PHY 242. Offered: F / As needed
PHY 445: Solid State Physics — 3 Credits
Introduction to crystal structures, electron waves in solids, semiconductors, and solid-state properties. Prerequisites: PHY 242 and PHY 301. Offered: S / As needed
PHY 455: Quantum Mechanics — 3 Credits
Introduction to quantum mechanics including Schrödinger equation and selected applications. Prerequisites: PHY 331 and PHY 345. Offered: S / As needed
